About the Research

Study Overview

This Executive MBA thesis at Swiss Business School examines how equity portfolio managers respond to sudden market information shocks – earnings surprises, analyst downgrades, and macro announcements – and whether a structured decision-support tool moderates behavioural biases in that process. All responses are anonymous and used solely for academic purposes.

Approx. 15 – 20 minutes Fully anonymised 24 market scenarios across 3 blocks Target: equity portfolio managers

Participation Criteria

Who Should Participate?

  • Equity portfolio manager with 2+ years of experience
  • Responsibility for portfolio construction or allocation decisions
  • Familiarity with interpreting market news and analyst reports

Participation is voluntary and fully anonymous. No personally identifiable information is collected.

Survey Format

What to Expect

Eight realistic investment scenarios per block, drawn from actual market events. Each scenario presents a stock profile, a current price, and a news headline. You indicate how you would adjust your position on a five-point scale. Some scenarios include a quantitative shock summary; others show the headline only.
